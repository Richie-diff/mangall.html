MANGA RECOMMENDATIONS

What should I start with?

For Beginners

Vinland saga

Vinland Saga is a historical manga series written and illustrated by Makoto Yukimura. It started serialization in 2005 and is published by Kodansha. The series is heavily inspired by real historical events and Norse mythology, focusing on Vikings, war, and revenge. The story follows Thorfinn, a young warrior from Iceland, who seeks revenge against the man who killed his father. However, as he grows up, he begins to question the nature of violence, war, and his own purpose in life.

Death Note

Death Note is a psychological thriller manga written by Tsugumi Ohba and illustrated by Takeshi Obata. It was serialized in Weekly Shōnen Jump from 2003 to 2006 and has since become one of the most iconic and influential manga series. The story follows Light Yagami, a highly intelligent high school student who discovers a mysterious notebook called the Death Note. This notebook allows its user to kill anyone simply by writing their name in it while picturing their face. Determined to create a utopia free of crime, Light takes on the alias Kira and starts eliminating criminals. However, his actions attract the attention of the world’s greatest detective, known only as L, leading to a high-stakes battle of wits between the two geniuses.

One Punch Man

One Punch Man is an action-comedy superhero manga created by ONE as a webcomic in 2009. Due to its popularity, it was later redrawn with professional illustrations by Yusuke Murata and serialized in Tonari no Young Jump (2012–present). The series is known for its mix of intense action, satire, and humor. The story follows Saitama, a hero who has trained so hard that he can defeat any enemy with a single punch. However, because of his overwhelming strength, he has grown bored of fighting and longs for a real challenge. Despite his immense power, he struggles with recognition, as he is often overlooked by the Hero Association and society.

For Intermediate

Chainsaw man

Chainsaw Man is a dark fantasy, action-horror manga written and illustrated by Tatsuki Fujimoto. It was serialized in Weekly Shōnen Jump from 2018 to 2020 (Part 1: Public Safety Arc) and later continued in Shōnen Jump+ from 2022 onward (Part 2: Academy Arc). Known for its brutal action, unpredictable storytelling, and dark humor, Chainsaw Man has gained a massive following. The manga follows Denji, a young man living in extreme poverty who works as a devil hunter to pay off his deceased father's debt to the yakuza. His only companion is Pochita, a small chainsaw devil. When Denji is betrayed and killed, Pochita sacrifices himself to revive Denji, granting him the ability to transform into Chainsaw Man, a human-devil hybrid with chainsaws coming out of his arms and head.

Tokyo ghoul

Tokyo Ghoul is a dark fantasy, horror, and psychological thriller manga written and illustrated by Sui Ishida. It was serialized in Weekly Young Jump from 2011 to 2014, followed by its sequel, Tokyo Ghoul:re (2014–2018). The series is famous for its intense action, deep character development, and thought-provoking themes about identity and morality. The story takes place in an alternate Tokyo where Ghouls, flesh-eating humanoid creatures, secretly live among humans. These ghouls look like normal people but must consume human flesh to survive. The government, through an organization called the CCG (Commission of Counter Ghoul), hunts them down. The protagonist, Ken Kaneki, is an ordinary college student who loves books. His life changes drastically when he goes on a date with Rize Kamishiro, who turns out to be a Ghoul. She attacks him, but an accident leads to her death, and Kaneki is saved by an emergency organ transplant—using Rize's organs. This transforms him into a half-Ghoul, forcing him to live in both the human and ghoul worlds. Now, Kaneki struggles to accept his monstrous nature while trying to maintain his humanity. He finds refuge at Anteiku, a café run by peaceful ghouls, and meets Touka Kirishima, a strong-willed ghoul who helps guide him. Meanwhile, the CCG relentlessly hunts ghouls, leading to brutal conflicts.

Beck

Beck: Mongolian Chop Squad is a music, coming-of-age, and slice-of-life manga written and illustrated by Harold Sakuishi. It was serialized in Monthly Shōnen Magazine from 1999 to 2008 and is highly regarded for its realistic depiction of the struggles of an aspiring rock band. The story follows Yukio "Koyuki" Tanaka, a 14-year-old ordinary teenager who feels stuck in a boring and directionless life. That changes when he saves a strange dog named Beck, owned by Ryusuke Minami, a gifted guitarist recently returned from the U.S. Ryusuke introduces Koyuki to rock music, igniting his passion for it. Koyuki starts learning the guitar, and with time, he joins Ryusuke's band, BECK. The story follows their journey from playing in small clubs to aiming for global success while facing obstacles such as rival bands, industry corruption, and personal struggles.

For Advanced

Climber

Climber (Kokou no Hito, 孤高の人) is a psychological, drama, and sports manga about mountain climbing, written by Yoshio Nabeta and illustrated by Shinichi Sakamoto. It was serialized from 2007 to 2012 in Young Jump and is loosely based on the real-life Japanese mountaineer Buntarō Katō. The story follows Mori Buntarō, a loner and social outcast who has difficulty connecting with others. After being challenged to climb his school’s building by a classmate, he unexpectedly discovers a passion for climbing. Unlike most people, Mori is drawn to solitary ascents, tackling dangerous mountains completely alone. As he progresses, he faces harsh natural elements, life-threatening challenges, and deep psychological struggles. His journey transforms from a physical challenge to an intense mental and emotional battle, exploring themes of loneliness, obsession, and the human will to overcome impossible odds.

Innocent

Innocent is a historical manga written and illustrated by Shin'ichi Sakamoto. It was serialized from 2013 to 2015, followed by its sequel Innocent Rouge, which ran from 2015 to 2020. The manga is known for its detailed artwork, dark themes, and historical accuracy. Set in 18th-century France, the story follows Charles-Henri Sanson, a historical figure who served as the official executioner of Paris during the years leading up to the French Revolution. Coming from a family of executioners, Charles is forced into a life he despises, torn between his personal morals and his family's duty to serve justice through executions. As the story unfolds, Charles undergoes intense psychological and emotional struggles, grappling with his role in society and the brutality of the legal system. The manga also explores the life of his younger sister, Marie-Joseph Sanson, who plays a major role in shaping history through her own ambitions and defiance against societal norms.

Land of the Lustrous

Land of the Lustrous (Houseki no Kuni) is a fantasy and action manga written and illustrated by Haruko Ichikawa. It has been serialized since 2012 and is known for its unique setting, philosophical themes, and deep character development. The story takes place in a distant future where humanoid gemstone beings called the Lustrous inhabit a world that has been devastated by meteor strikes. These beings are made of precious minerals and do not age or die in a conventional sense. However, they are constantly under attack by the Lunarians, mysterious celestial beings who seek to shatter them and use their remains as decorations. The protagonist, Phosphophyllite (Phos), is one of the weakest of the Lustrous due to their brittle nature. Desperate to find their purpose, Phos is assigned to create an encyclopedia but longs for a more important role, such as fighting. As the story progresses, Phos embarks on a journey of self-discovery, change, and the painful cost of growth—both physically and mentally.
