GENRES OF MANGA

What kind of Mangas are out there?

Shōnen (少年) – For Young Boys

Shōnen manga is aimed at young male readers (typically ages 12–18) and often features action, adventure, friendship, and perseverance. The main protagonist is usually a young boy striving to improve himself. Common Themes: Battles and tournaments, Training and growth, Strong friendships and rivalries, Comedy elements. Popular Examples: Dragon Ball – A martial arts adventure featuring Goku’s journey from childhood to becoming Earth’s strongest fighter. Naruto – A young ninja, Naruto Uzumaki, dreams of becoming Hokage while dealing with the challenges of his past. One Piece – Follows Monkey D. Luffy and his pirate crew searching for the legendary treasure, the "One Piece." My Hero Academia – A world where people have superpowers (quirks), and the protagonist, Deku, aims to become the greatest hero.

Shōjo (少女) – For Young Girls

Shōjo manga is aimed at young female readers (typically ages 10–18) and often focuses on romance, emotions, and relationships. Common Themes: Love triangles and drama, Emotional storytelling, School life and friendships, Beautiful artwork with expressive characters. Popular Examples: Sailor Moon – A magical girl series where Usagi Tsukino fights evil while balancing her normal teenage life. Fruits Basket – A romantic drama about a girl who gets involved with a family cursed to transform into zodiac animals. Ouran High School Host Club – A comedic romance where a girl accidentally joins a school’s elite host club. Cardcaptor Sakura – A young girl discovers magical cards and must collect them to prevent disasters.

Seinen (青年) – For Adult Men

Seinen manga is targeted at adult men (18+), featuring mature themes, deeper storytelling, and complex characters. Common Themes: Psychological depth, Dark and realistic settings, Action with intense violence, Political and social issues. Popular Examples: Berserk – A dark fantasy about a warrior named Guts, who seeks revenge against his former friend-turned-enemy. Ghost in the Shell – A cyberpunk sci-fi series about cyborgs, hacking, and futuristic crime-solving. Tokyo Ghoul – A horror-thriller about a boy who turns into a flesh-eating ghoul after an accident. Vinland Saga – A historical action manga about Vikings, war, and revenge.

Josei (女性) – For Adult Women

Josei manga is aimed at adult female readers (18+), featuring realistic romantic relationships and slice-of-life stories. Common Themes: Mature romance, Workplace and adult life struggles, Family relationships, Less fantasy, more realism. Popular Examples: Nana – Follows two women named Nana navigating their careers, friendships, and love lives. Paradise Kiss – A coming-of-age romance about a high school girl discovering the fashion industry. Honey and Clover – A slice-of-life story about college students dealing with love and future aspirations. March Comes in Like a Lion – A drama about a young professional shogi player dealing with personal struggles.

Isekai (異世界) – "Other World" Genre

Isekai manga features characters who are transported to or reincarnated in a different world, usually a fantasy or game-like universe. Common Themes: Reincarnation or summoning to another world, RPG-style leveling up and magic, Protagonist gaining special powers. Popular Examples: Re:Zero – Starting Life in Another World – A boy is transported to a fantasy world where he relives his death repeatedly. That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime – A man is reborn as a slime in a magical world and becomes a powerful leader. Sword Art Online – Players get trapped inside a virtual MMORPG and must fight their way out. Mushoku Tensei – A man gets reincarnated in a fantasy world and decides to live a better life.

Mecha (メカ) – Robot Genre

Mecha manga revolves around giant robots, futuristic warfare, and technology-driven battles. Common Themes: Giant humanoid robots piloted by humans, Futuristic and military settings, Conflicts between humanity and machines. Popular Examples: Mobile Suit Gundam – A franchise focusing on wars fought using mecha suits. Neon Genesis Evangelion – A psychological sci-fi mecha series exploring human emotions and war. Code Geass – A mix of political intrigue, mecha battles, and strategic warfare. Gurren Lagann – An over-the-top mecha adventure about breaking limits and freedom.

Horror (ホラー) – Horror and Supernatural

Horror manga includes eerie, disturbing, and supernatural elements designed to scare readers. Common Themes: Psychological horror, Ghosts and monsters, Body horror and grotesque imagery. Popular Examples: Junji Ito’s Uzumaki – A psychological horror about a town obsessed with spirals. Tokyo Ghoul – A dark horror story about humans coexisting with flesh-eating creatures. The Promised Neverland – Orphans discover they are raised as food for monsters and try to escape. Parasyte – An alien invasion story where parasites take control of human bodies.

Slice of Life (日常) – Everyday Life

Slice of Life manga focuses on the mundane, everyday experiences of characters, often with comedy or emotional depth. Common Themes: School life and friendships, Comedy and lighthearted moments, Personal growth and self-discovery. Popular Examples: Barakamon – A calligrapher moves to a rural village and learns life lessons from its quirky residents. March Comes in Like a Lion – A deep, emotional story about a young shogi player overcoming depression. K-On! – A lighthearted comedy about high school girls forming a music band. My Dress-Up Darling – A mix of romance and slice-of-life where a boy helps a girl with her cosplay hobby.

Sports (スポーツ) – Sports and Competition

Sports manga centers around athletic competitions, teamwork, and character growth. Common Themes: Underdog stories, Hard work and perseverance, Rivalries and teamwork. Popular Examples: Haikyuu!! – A volleyball manga following a short player who dreams of becoming a great spiker. Kuroko’s Basketball – High school basketball with exaggerated yet exciting moves. Hajime no Ippo – A boxing series about a timid boy who becomes a professional boxer. Blue Lock – A unique take on soccer, where strikers must compete in a brutal elimination program.
